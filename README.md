# Software_Mailing_Anlaytics_D
Customer Purchase Propensity & Marketing Attribution Analysis

Problem:
Marketing campaigns were impacted by untargeted outreach, resulting in lower response rates and inefficient use of marketing resources. The business needed a way to identify high-value customers and improve campaign effectiveness.

Approach:
Analyzed customer and campaign data to understand purchasing behavior
Performed data cleaning and exploratory analysis to uncover patterns
Built a purchase propensity model to estimate likelihood of conversion
Applied customer segmentation techniques to group customers with similar behaviors
Evaluated model performance using classification and lift-based metrics

Language: R
Methods: Classification, Clustering, Predictive Analytics
Models: Logistic Regression, Random Forest, K-Means
Evaluation: ROC/AUC, Gains & Lift Analysis

Outcome:
Identified high-value customer segments for targeted marketing
Supported improved campaign attribution and decision-making
Enabled an estimated 15–25% improvement in marketing response efficiency
